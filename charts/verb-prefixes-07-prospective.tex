\section{Prospective aspect prefixes \textit{w-g-g̱-}}\label{sec:prosp}

\begin{multicols}{2}
\noindent
Prospective aspect – also inaccurately called ‘future tense’ by analogy with English – is expressed with three prefixes \fm{w-}, \fm{g-}, and \fm{g̱-}.
The \fm{w-} /\ipa{ʷ}/ irrealis prefix is an allomorph of the irrealis prefix that occurs with the prospective aspect and with comparatives.
The \fm{g-} /\ipa{k}/ conjugation prefix is identified as a conjugation prefix although it is used for the prospective aspect regardless of the verb’s conjugation class membership.
This is similar to the use of the \fm{n-} /\ipa{n}/ conjugation prefix for all prospective aspect forms regardless of the verb’s conjugation class.
The \fm{g̱-} /\ipa{q}/ modal prefix is associated with modality, being found also in the hortative mood and the potential modality.
The \fm{g̱-} modal prefix is homophonous with the \fm{g̱-} conjugation prefix but the two can occur together so they are not identical.
\end{multicols}
